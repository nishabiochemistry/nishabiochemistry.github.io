Biography:

    Dr. Nisha Singh is an Indian biologist. Her research focuses on the molecular analysis of parasitic pathogen and host-pathogen interactions viz. Leishmania. She is Assistant Professor for Biochemistry (since 2013) at the HNB Garhwal University and leads the Molecular-Immunology Laboratory. Translational research in her laboratory is focused on developing novel therapeutic strategies and vaccines against parasitic infections such as leishmaniasis. She received numerous awards and is a member of several national and international academies, scientific societies and committees. She was invited as Life Science Expert by The Swedish Institute, Government of Sweden to participate in Expert Visitor’s programme 2019 edition at Stockholm, Sweden with the aim to strengthen ties between Sweden and India in the Life Science sector. She is a recipient of the ICMR Award for Biomedical Research from Indian Council of Medical Research, Government of India; A. Krishnamurthy Award from SBC(I)- IISc. Bangaluru, Start-Up Research Grant from UGC; multiple ICGEB Grants and several travel grants. She has acted as Reviewer for various prestigious journals such as IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, Journal of Scientific Research etc. and is a member of IQAC Task Group, Board of Studies (BOS-Biochemistry), DAIP Committee (Biochemistry), School Board and Academic Council at HNB Garhwal University.

    She received her both B.Sc. (Biology) and M.Sc. (Biochemistry) degrees from University of Lucknow, India and her Ph.D. in Biochemistry from Banaras Hindu University, India.
